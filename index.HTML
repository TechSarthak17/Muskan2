Chhath Puja - The Festival of Sun God

Celebrating Devotion, Purity, and Gratitude towards Nature

What is Chhath Puja?

Chhath Puja is an ancient Hindu festival dedicated to the worship of the Sun God (Surya) and Chhathi Maiya. It is mainly celebrated in Bihar, Jharkhand, Uttar Pradesh, and Nepal. The festival expresses gratitude to the Sun for sustaining life on Earth and requests health, prosperity, and well-being.

History and Significance

Chhath Puja is one of the oldest Vedic festivals. It is believed to have started during the times of Mahabharata and Ramayana. According to legends, Draupadi and the Pandavas performed Chhath Puja to regain their lost kingdom. The rituals signify purity, devotion, and discipline.

Rituals and Celebration

  • Day 1 – Nahay Khay: Devotees take a holy bath and prepare pure vegetarian food.
  • Day 2 – Kharna: Devotees observe a fast for the entire day and break it after sunset.
  • Day 3 – Sandhya Arghya: Devotees offer evening prayers to the setting sun on riverbanks.
  • Day 4 – Usha Arghya: Morning offerings to the rising sun mark the end of the fast.

Why Chhath Puja is Celebrated

The festival symbolizes gratitude toward the Sun God for energy, light, and life. It promotes environmental consciousness, health benefits through fasting, and the importance of family unity and devotion.

Customs and Traditions

Devotees stand in water to offer prayers, use bamboo baskets for offerings, and avoid using modern items like plastic. Women mostly observe the fast and maintain purity throughout the ritual.
